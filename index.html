Naucratis (Kom Gi'eif)

Overview

Naucratis was the first and only permanent Greek colony in Egypt, acting as a pivotal trading hub between Greek and Egyptian civilizations.

History

Founded in the 7th century BC. Pharaoh Amasis II granted it exclusive trading rights, making it the gateway for Greek goods before Alexandria arose.

Key Facts

Famous for the "Hellenion" sanctuary and distinctive pottery. Located near modern-day Kom Gi'eif in Beheira.

Monastery of al-Baramus

Overview

The Monastery of the al-Baramos is located in Wadi al-Natrun. First monastic establishment in 340AD.

History

Home to saints like Moses the Black. Rebuilt after multiple attacks.

Key Facts

Named "Baramus" meaning "The Romans". Contains ancient ruins nearby.

Citadel of Qaitbay in Rosetta

Overview

Historic fort on the Nile, built in 1470 AD by Sultan Qaytbay to protect against Ottomans.

History

Used by Ottomans, then French (Fort Julien). Rosetta Stone found here in 1799.

Key Facts

Built using recycled ancient temple blocks. Restored in the 1980s.

Monastery of Saint Macarius

Overview

Founded by St. Macarius (360 AD). Official residence of Coptic patriarchs for centuries.

History

Sacked by Berbers (49 martyrs). Restored massively in modern times.

Key Facts

Housed a great library. Contains the Church of the Forty-Nine Martyrs.

The Rosetta Stone

The Discovery

Discovered in 1799 in Qaitbay Fort. The key to deciphering Hieroglyphs.

The Replica

Original is in London. Rosetta Museum hosts a detailed replica.

Significance

Text in Hieroglyphs, Demotic, and Greek. Deciphered by Champollion in 1822.
